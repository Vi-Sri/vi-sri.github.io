# Four-month revival plan

**Window:** July 20–November 17, 2026  
**North star:** Become a recognizable, trusted voice at the intersection of computation, evolution, morphogenesis, and complex systems, not merely a blog with more traffic.

“Famous” is not a controllable four-month outcome. Reputation signals are. This plan optimizes for work that is memorable, citable, discussable, and connected to reproducible artifacts.

## The scoreboard

Do not optimize for raw page views, posting streaks, or social impressions without downstream reading and conversation.

## The publishing rhythm

- **Tuesday:** one compact field note (500–900 words, one idea, one figure or example).
- **Every second Thursday:** one flagship essay (2,500–4,000 words, primary sources, original visual, explicit limitations).
- **Friday:** one build log from a simulation or research artifact.
- **Monthly:** one live or recorded research conversation with a domain expert.
- **End of each month:** publish a “map of what changed” that links new work back into the concept graph.

This is deliberately fewer long essays than a content-marketing plan. The quality bar is closer to a small paper than a generic blog post.

### Flagship essays

5. **Evolution Without a Fitness Function: What Open-Ended Search Is Actually Asking For**  
   Contribution: compare objective-driven evolutionary computing with novelty, quality diversity, and open-endedness.
6. **What Does a Cell Know? Agency Across Biological Scales**  
   Contribution: propose operational tests for memory, preference, error correction, and model-based control without anthropomorphism.

### Paper candidate gate

Proceed only if all answers are “yes”:

1. Is there one sentence stating the claim without fashionable umbrella terms?
2. Is there a real gap in the primary literature, not merely a gap in this blog?
3. Can a result disconfirm the claim?
4. Is the baseline comparison fair and reproducible?
5. Can the work be released without NVIDIA-confidential information, code, data, or employer conflicts?

If the empirical gate fails, write a rigorous position or survey paper instead of disguising synthesis as a novel experiment.

8. **When Does a Society Compute? A Mechanism-First Account of Collective Intelligence**  
   Contribution: connect distributed computation with institutions while explicitly testing the limits of the analogy.

### Preprint launch sequence

1. Obtain employer/publication clearance where required.
2. Ask three reviewers with different expertise to attack the claim, methods, and interpretation.
3. Freeze code, environment, seeds, data provenance, and an archival release.
4. Publish the preprint and artifact before promotional summaries so every claim has a stable source.
5. Release an accessible essay, technical thread, short talk, and figures over one week.
6. Respond to substantive criticism; log changes publicly; do not debate drive-by reactions.

## Venue map, not a submission promise

Select a venue only after the contribution exists. Policies and calls must be rechecked on official pages before submission.

- [Artificial Life (MIT Press)](https://direct.mit.edu/artl): strongest fit for synthetic or simulated systems that advance understanding of living processes.
- [Complex Systems](https://www.complex-systems.com/about/): broad fit across mathematics, physics, computer science, and biology when simple components produce complex collective behavior.
- [IEEE Transactions on Evolutionary Computation](https://cis.ieee.org/publications/t-evolutionary-computation): fit for a substantial, technically evaluated evolutionary-computation contribution rather than a conceptual essay.
- [International Society for Artificial Life conferences](https://alife.org/conferences/): use the 2026 meeting for community learning if practical; target 2027 for new work because the 2026 submission deadline has passed.
- GECCO 2027: likely fit for rigorous evolutionary algorithms, quality diversity, genetic programming, or open-ended search; wait for the official 2027 call.

The four-month goal is one defensible preprint. Journal acceptance is outside the window and outside the author’s control.

## Stop-doing list

- Do not publish empty title pages or generic AI-generated summaries.
- Do not create dozens of near-synonymous tags.
- Do not write about a famous scientist unless the piece advances a question.
- Do not cross-post identical full text without a canonical link and a reason.
- Do not promote in communities before learning their norms and contributing there.
- Do not let the newsletter, graph, or visual polish delay the next excellent argument.
